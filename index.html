A little about me

I've been a professional technical writer and communicator since 2010 and am currently a graduate student at George Mason University, where I'm pursuing a Master's of English in Professional Writing & Rhetoric (PWR). I take pride in my craft, in which I analyze and enhance textual artifacts to empower audiences of all types.

More about me

What I do

In my current position, I am the Sr. Technical Writer on a large government contract that provides death benefits and legal processing services for participants and beneficiaries of the Federal Retirement Thrift Investment Board (FRTIB) Thrift Savings Plan (TSP), as well as provides agency technical support to help agencies troubleshoot their payroll submissions. On this contract, I help shape, manage, and maintain a wide variety of user-focused process documentation (e.g., SOP manuals, policies, guidelines, desk guides, reports, job aids, administrative forms), as well as consult on other detail-oriented assignments where technical/professional communication expertise is needed.

My skills

During my time as a technical writer, I've become highly proficient at using the very basic resources available to me, such as Microsoft Office, HTML/CSS, and the Windows Snipping Tool to craft engaging and empowering user documentation. Even with these limitations, I am constantly seeking more efficient and effective methods of communicating technical information. As such, I have become proficient in using Adobe Acrobat, Illustrator, and Photoshop, as well as Camtasia and SnagIt from TechSmith. Right now, I am working to become fluent in JavaScript and learning how to use Adobe RoboHelp and Framemaker.
